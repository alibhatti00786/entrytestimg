1About King Edward Medical University (KEMU) Lahore

King Edward Medical University, located in the heart of Lahore, is Pakistan's oldest and most prestigious public medical institution. Founded in 1860, KEMU has been producing some of the country's finest doctors for over 160 years. It is affiliated with Mayo Hospital — one of Pakistan's largest public teaching hospitals — giving students unmatched clinical exposure from day one.

KEMU consistently ranks as the top medical university in Punjab and is recognised by PMDC, HEC, and WHO. Its MBBS program runs for 5 years, followed by a 1-year mandatory house job. The university is a dream destination for pre-medical students across Pakistan — which is exactly why its merit is the highest among all public sector medical colleges in Punjab.

Admissions to KEMU's MBBS and BDS programs are managed through the University of Health Sciences (UHS) Lahore, which is the admitting body for all public sector medical colleges in Punjab. All applications, merit lists, and seat allocations go through the UHS portal.

KEMU does not accept direct applications. All MBBS admissions are processed through UHS Lahore at uhs.edu.pk. Always follow UHS for official announcements, merit lists, and deadlines.

4How Merit is Calculated for KEMU

KEMU uses the standard UHS merit formula applied to all Punjab public medical colleges. There is no separate KEMU test — your MDCAT score, FSc marks, and Matric marks are combined as follows:

6KEMU Application Process Step-by-Step

KEMU MBBS admissions are fully online through the UHS Lahore portal. No hard copy applications are accepted. Here is the complete process:

  1. Read the UHS Prospectus for the current session — download it from uhs.edu.pk. It contains the official merit formula, fee breakdown, and all seat categories.
  2. Register on the UHS Admission Portal at uhs.edu.pk — create an account with your name, guardian's name, email, and password.
  3. Choose your program — select MBBS, BDS, or both, and choose your seat categories (open merit, self-finance, quota).
  4. Fill your academic details — enter your Matric marks, FSc marks, and MDCAT score. Your aggregate is calculated automatically by the system.
  5. Upload required documents — Matric certificate, FSc certificate or result, MDCAT result card, domicile certificate, CNIC/B-Form, passport-size photo.
  6. Pay the application processing fee — PKR 2,000 per program, payable at any Bank of Punjab branch or via online banking.
  7. Submit your college preference list — rank KEMU and other colleges in your preferred order. Higher aggregate students get first choice.
  8. Check UHS merit lists — UHS releases multiple provisional and final merit lists from October/November onward. Check your status on the portal regularly.
  9. Deposit fees before the deadline — if selected, you must pay your fee by the UHS deadline or lose your seat. Your seat goes to the next student on the merit list.
Admissions typically open in October/November following MDCAT results. UHS releases multiple merit lists (often 5–7) — if you are not selected in the first list, do not lose hope. Keep checking until the final list.

8Tips to Secure a Seat at KEMU

Getting into KEMU requires a near-perfect aggregate. Here is what actually moves the needle:

Target 165+ in MDCAT

With MDCAT carrying 50% weight, scoring 165 out of 200 (82.5%) gives you a huge advantage. Combined with strong FSc marks, this puts you comfortably above KEMU's 93.7% closing merit.

Don't Neglect FSc Marks

FSc carries 40% weight — the second largest component. Students with 90%+ FSc marks have a built-in 4% advantage over those with 80% FSc marks, even with the same MDCAT score.

Apply to Multiple Seat Categories

If you are eligible for a quota seat (AJK, Balochistan, GB), apply for both open merit and the quota. Quota competition pools are smaller, improving your chances significantly.

Rank KEMU First in Your Preference List

UHS assigns seats based on merit and preference order. If KEMU is not ranked first in your list, you may miss a seat even if your aggregate is high enough — always put your top-choice college first.

Monitor All Merit Lists

UHS releases multiple merit lists. Many students get selected in the 3rd, 4th or 5th list after higher-ranked students miss their fee deadlines. Check the Tayari Shayari merit lists page regularly.

9FAQs — KEMU Admissions 2026

What aggregate do I need to get into KEMU in 2026?
Based on 2024 data, KEMU's open merit closing aggregate was approximately 93.7%. Given the rising trend (91.67% in 2022 → 93.7% in 2024), students aiming for KEMU in 2026 should target 94% or above to be safe. This requires very strong MDCAT and FSc scores. Use the aggregate calculator to see where you stand.
How do I apply for KEMU MBBS admission?
KEMU does not accept direct applications. All MBBS admissions are handled by UHS (University of Health Sciences) Lahore at uhs.edu.pk. You must register on the UHS online portal, fill your academic details, upload documents, pay the PKR 2,000 processing fee, and submit your college preference list. Admissions typically open in October/November each year after MDCAT results.
How many MBBS seats does KEMU have?
KEMU has approximately 370 total MBBS seats per session. This includes roughly 300 open merit seats for Punjab domicile students, about 37 self-finance seats, 1 reserved seat each for AJK, Balochistan, and Gilgit-Baltistan, and 10% of total seats for foreign/overseas candidates. Unfilled quota seats are transferred to open merit.
What is the annual MBBS fee at KEMU?
For Punjab domicile students on open merit seats, the 1st year tuition fee is approximately PKR 100,000, with subsequent years at around PKR 70,000–80,000 per year. The full 5-year program costs approximately PKR 380,000–400,000 in total — far less than private medical colleges. Fees are set by the Government of Punjab and may change each session.
